GENERAL ENGLISH

The General English Course covers all aspects of the language: reading, writing, listening, and speaking. It aims to enhance overall proficiency without specialized focus. This program develops grammar, expands vocabulary, and boosts confidence in everyday communication.
GENERAL ENGLISH PROGRAM BY LEVELS

Beginner

This is the stage in which a person becomes familiar with the basics of the language, mastering basic phrases, simple sentence structures and basic rules of grammar. At this stage, it is important to focus on the basics and develop the basic ability to understand and use English in simple communication situations. For a beginning learner, it is important to have patience, consistency and regular practice to gradually improve your language skills.

Elementary

At this level, the student already has basic vocabulary and grammar for everyday and everyday communication on familiar topics. He understands some of the rules of the English language and can construct simple sentences and questions of various types. This student can speak in the present, past and future tense, can introduce himself, talk about himself, his family, his work day, and his hobbies.

The student understands the slow speech of native speakers and texts adapted to the level. Knowledge is enough to support a minimal conversation in English, but there are not yet enough resources to understand fluent speech, speak on more complex, abstract topics and express all your thoughts in English.

The program at this level involves expanding basic vocabulary, “deepening” already familiar fundamental topics in grammar and mastering new grammatical structures.

Pre-Intermediate

At this level there is already a knowledge base: the student can communicate on familiar and everyday topics in the present, past and future and understands the differences between the various aspects of these tenses. He has sufficient vocabulary to not only discuss surrounding objects and simple actions, but also evaluate them, analyze them and express his point of view.

It is still difficult for a student at the Pre-intermediate level to use more complex phrases in speech: he is limited to simple sentences and vocabulary, although he can understand the meaning of what he read, understands most of the information if the interlocutor speaks slowly on a familiar topic. When traveling abroad, the level is enough to maintain a conversation in typical everyday situations, to use English for everyday communication

Intermediate

This is the average level at which all previously acquired knowledge is generalized and deepened. Students are able to select and actively use all basic tenses and constructions in speech, understand the difference between them and their place in the system of English grammar. Having an Intermediate level, a person understands the speech of native speakers and communicates confidently in most situations, constructing more complex sentences. He has a vocabulary on many topics: both everyday and more abstract, and knows how to describe impressions, events, state and justify his opinions and plans for the future, knows how to react in various situations.

An average level of English is the main requirement for admission to many foreign universities and when applying for a job in international companies. With the Intermediate level, you can pass interviews in English, feel confident abroad and begin preparing for international exams.

Some topics that are included in the program for the Intermediate level were already present at previous levels. Here we systematize and deepen knowledge, and of course, expand existing knowledge of vocabulary and add new grammatical structures.

Upper-Intermediate

A student whose level is Upper Intermediate has enough language resources to maintain a conversation in English on almost any topic (except for highly specialized ones) and understand the speech of native speakers at a normal pace. People with this level understand grammatical nuances, are familiar with all tenses of English, with all types of conditional sentences and other complex structures. They can construct complex sentences rich in high-level vocabulary and grammar, are familiar with phrasal verbs and can choose more “advanced” synonyms for simple words, understand 90 percent or more of what they read in the original, and can compose texts of different formats and topics.

It would seem: what else is needed? But students with this level often come to us for training, because the main problem of the level is a large amount of passive knowledge. The goal of the Upper Intermediate level programs is to activate passive knowledge from previous levels, systematize it, deepen it and, of course, expand the vocabulary.

Advanced

At an advanced level, the student speaks English almost as fluently as his native language: he can communicate on various topics using complex grammatical structures and advanced vocabulary, knows and puts into practice phrasal verbs, stable combinations, and practically does not experience a lack of linguistic resources when expressing your thoughts. Students at this level are distinguished by the fact that they are able to notice and correct their mistakes, understand the intricacies of contexts and the different meanings of words. They fully understand unadapted texts, they listen to audio and watch films in the original, only occasionally turning to the dictionary.
